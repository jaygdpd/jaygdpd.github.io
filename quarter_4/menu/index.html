Reflection

I found menu design to be overall a fun, albeit relatively repetitive process. Most of the techniques and skills used for the notepad design process was used here, with the addition of photo editing of food items. I appreciate the reduced sketching process, since it gave me more time to work on the final product. I liked how the project was separated into very clear stages, like the sketching stage, the menu item design stage, the photo-editing stage, and the final layout stage. For photo-editing, I used masks to lighten up portions of the food to make it more glossy and lit-up. Mr. Sauve's surprise announcement that our designs had all been rejected gave us the opportunity to work on design 2. Sadly, in real life the graphic design process often requires rejection and redoing. I liked my two final products, and I'm proud of the work I did this unit.
